Title
The Life Story of Dr. José Rizal

Page 1
 José Rizal was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna, into a well-educated Filipino family that valued learning and discipline. From a young age, he showed remarkable intelligence, learning to read early and developing a passion for writing and education. His mother, Teodora Alonso, served as his first teacher and greatly influenced his intellectual growth. Rizal studied in Biñan, Laguna, and later at Ateneo Municipal de Manila, where he excelled academically. He continued his studies at the University of Santo Tomas, but due to discrimination from Spanish professors, he decided to pursue further education abroad.

Page 2
Rizal traveled to Spain and studied at the Universidad Central de Madrid, where he became a doctor specializing in ophthalmology, aiming to treat his mother’s eye condition. During his time in Europe, he also studied in France and Germany, gaining knowledge and learning multiple languages. While abroad, Rizal became more aware of the injustices experienced by Filipinos under Spanish colonial rule. Instead of using violence, he chose to fight through his writings. He wrote Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, novels that exposed the abuses of Spanish authorities and awakened a sense of nationalism among Filipinos.

Page 3
Because of his influence, Rizal was considered a threat by the Spanish government. He was arrested and exiled to Dapitan, where he continued to serve the community by teaching, practicing medicine, and helping improve local life. In 1896, he was accused of involvement in the Philippine Revolution, arrested, and brought back to Manila. He was sentenced to death and executed on December 30, 1896, at Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park). His death inspired Filipinos to continue the fight for freedom, and he is now remembered as the national hero of the Philippines, symbolizing intelligence, courage, and peaceful resistance.

Title
The Life Story of Andres Bonifacio

Page 1
Andres Bonifacio was born on November 30, 1863, in Tondo, Manila. Unlike other heroes, he did not come from a wealthy or highly educated family. After the death of his parents, Bonifacio had to stop studying and worked hard to support his younger siblings. Despite this, he educated himself by reading books about history, law, and the French Revolution. Through his experiences and self-learning, he developed a strong sense of patriotism and a desire to fight against injustice.

Page 2
As Spanish oppression in the Philippines continued, Bonifacio believed that peaceful reform was not enough to achieve freedom. Inspired by the ideas of nationalism, he founded the Katipunan, a secret revolutionary society that aimed to gain independence from Spain through armed struggle. He became known as the “Father of the Philippine Revolution” because of his leadership and courage. In 1896, Bonifacio led the movement that sparked the revolution, including the historic Cry of Pugad Lawin, where Filipinos tore their cedulas as a sign of resistance against Spanish rule.

Page 3
During the revolution, conflicts arose within the revolutionary government, leading to disagreements between Bonifacio and other leaders. He was later arrested and accused of treason. In 1897, Andres Bonifacio was executed in Maragondon, Cavite. Although his life ended tragically, his bravery and willingness to fight for freedom made him one of the greatest heroes of the Philippines. Today, he is remembered as a symbol of courage, leadership, and the Filipino people’s struggle for independence.

Title
The Life Story of Heneral Luna

Page 1
Antonio Luna was born on October 29, 1866, in Binondo, Manila. He was a highly educated Filipino who studied in Spain and earned a degree in pharmacy. He was also a scientist and became known for his research in chemistry. At first, Luna supported peaceful reforms like other Filipino intellectuals, but after witnessing the execution of Filipino patriots and the continued abuse under colonial rule, he became more determined to fight for the country’s freedom.

Page 2
When the Philippine Revolution and later the Philippine-American War began, Luna became one of the most skilled and disciplined military leaders of the Filipino forces. He served as a general and was known for his strict leadership, strong strategies, and desire to build a well-trained and organized army. He introduced military systems such as defensive trenches and strategic planning to strengthen the Filipino resistance. However, his strict personality caused conflicts with other soldiers and leaders.

Page 3
On June 5, 1899, Antonio Luna was assassinated in Cabanatuan, Nueva Ecija by fellow Filipino soldiers. His death weakened the Filipino army during the war. Despite his tragic end, Heneral Luna is remembered as a brave and brilliant military leader who fought with discipline and determination for Philippine independence.

Title
The Life Story of Lapu-Lapu

Page 1
Lapu-Lapu was a powerful chieftain of Mactan, Cebu, who lived during the early 16th century. He is known as one of the first Filipino heroes to resist foreign colonization. During this time, Spanish explorers, led by Ferdinand Magellan, arrived in the Philippines and began forming alliances with local rulers. While some leaders accepted Spanish authority, Lapu-Lapu chose to defend his land and people, refusing to submit to foreign control.

Page 2
In April 1521, Magellan attempted to assert his power over Mactan, but Lapu-Lapu resisted. This led to the Battle of Mactan on April 27, 1521. Lapu-Lapu and his warriors fought bravely using their knowledge of the terrain and local combat strategies. Despite the advanced weapons of the Spanish forces, Lapu-Lapu’s leadership and courage led to the defeat of Magellan, who was killed during the battle.

Page 3
The victory at Mactan became a historic moment, marking the first successful resistance against foreign invaders in the Philippines. Lapu-Lapu’s bravery symbolizes the Filipino spirit of independence and courage. Today, he is honored as a national hero, representing strength, leadership, and the willingness to fight for freedom and sovereignty.

Title
The Life Story of Apolinario Mabini

Page 1
Apolinario Mabini was born on July 23, 1864, in Tanauan, Batangas, into a humble and poor family. Despite their limited resources, Mabini showed determination to pursue his education. He worked hard to support himself while studying, often facing financial struggles. Through perseverance, he was able to study at the University of Santo Tomas, where he earned a degree in law. Mabini became known for his sharp intellect, discipline, and strong sense of justice, which later shaped his role in the Philippine Revolution.

Page 2
Mabini’s life changed when he suffered from paralysis, which left him unable to walk. Despite this condition, he remained mentally strong and continued to serve his country. During the revolution, he became the chief adviser of Emilio Aguinaldo and played a key role in organizing the revolutionary government. Because of his intelligence and influence, he was called the “Brains of the Revolution.” Mabini emphasized the importance of proper governance, discipline among leaders, and unity among Filipinos in achieving true independence.

Page 3
He strongly believed that freedom should not only be achieved but also properly managed through a just and disciplined government. Mabini opposed corruption and warned against leaders who sought personal power instead of national progress. Even when he was captured and exiled by the Americans, he remained firm in his principles and refused to compromise his beliefs. Today, Apolinario Mabini is remembered as a symbol of wisdom, integrity, and dedication to the Filipino nation.
